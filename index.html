About Me

Get to know me better

I didn't start with a plan. I started with curiosity — pulled apart code to see what moved, broke things more than I fixed them, and somewhere in that mess, found that I genuinely loved it.

My first real spark was audio. I got obsessed with how machines could understand emotion from sound — not rules, not logic, just raw signal. That led me down a rabbit hole of signal processing, deep learning, and eventually a paper accepted at RECCAP 2026. It wasn't glamorous. It was months of MFCC features, failed model runs, and one very stubborn wav2vec2 fine-tune.

Then IIT Delhi happened. Working on the SARCH project — building OCR and RAG pipelines over decades of scanned archaeological documents — taught me what "production-ready" actually means. Not just a model that works in a notebook, but a system that holds up when the data is messy, the requirements shift, and real researchers depend on it. I co-authored the paper. That one still feels surreal.

Now I'm deep into AI agents — LangGraph, MCP, autonomous systems that actually do things. I find it fascinating that we're at a point where you can wire up a reasoning loop and watch it make decisions. Scary and exciting in equal parts.

Outside all this, I play badminton, run a YouTube channel of vlogs and study content, and post tech discoveries on Instagram as @atrexplains. Still a BTech student at CSVTU, Bhilai — but the learning curve doesn't wait for a degree.
